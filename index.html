Museums I've Explored

The Museum of Science & Industry

Chicago, Illinois

The first museum I ever went to and what has certainly inspired my lifelong desire to learn and appreciate the carefully curated collections within cultural institutions. As a young child I entered this museum and knew this was the happiest I had ever felt, and I had been to Disney before. We traversed through a submarine, sat under the giant dome and learned about the universe, walked among preserved parts of human bodies from all ages, and I loved every minute of it.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

New York City, New York

Although this museum is known for being one of the best collections of art, history, and culture, admission is donation based. I walked up the main stairs and was greeted by George Washington crossing the Delaware River. I saw portraits of Van Gogh, Monat’s lillies, Italian sculptures, crown jewels from lost civilizations, cracked pottery from the ancient city of Pompeo and so, so, so many people. My favorite visit ended with looking out at the fall colors of Central Park from the bay windows near the reflection pools, and I watched a man get down on one knee and ask his partner to marry him. A memorable day for all.

The Museum of Modern Art

New York City, New York

This visit started with a lecture on black representation within award winning documentaries. A panel of experts reviewed and discussed the complexities of a film genre that I had never considered before. Afterwards, I passed numerous paintings of chicken noodle soup on my way to look at a Starry Night. Crowds swelled around the surprisingly small painting and we each took our turn jumping in front of it for pictures. We walked around and enjoyed the rest of the floor we were on, which included very pleasant interactive lazer shows.

American Museum of Western Art

Denver, Colorado

The largest collection of indiginous art under one roof, rumor has it was almost completely donated by one person. The museum sits on the quiet side of the mountain slope and has friendly staff to welcome you in. I learned a lot about Native American’s and their everyday life prior to the near genocide the colonizers inflicted upon their people. Interestingly, there were quite a few depictions of lone cowboys and outlaws who had allied with many of the western tribes. The landscapes from the artist’s were created with such detail, I felt like I could feel the sun through the trees or hear the hooves of a horse walking on soil. Very different from the heavily eurocentric dominated museums I have visited before.

The National Museum of African American History & Culture

Washington D.C, U.S

Like so many other wonderful museums at the nation’s capital, The NMAAHC is entirely free to the public. It’s immediately striking the distinction of this building compared to the rest of the National Mall. The building, I learned, was designed and inspired African American and West African themes in the form, material, and color of the exterior and interior. I loved the excitement from all the school aged children inside the halls. There were beautiful celebratory moments that juxtaposed with deeply saddening and tragic realities that I had truthfully never heard about in any of my history studies.

The St. Louis Art Museum

St. Louis, Missouri

The property the museum sits upon is so stunning it cannot be unmentioned. The sprawling hills and foilage with statues peaking in and out provide serenity every season of the year. The museum has several different era’s and is relatively easy to navigate. I was very pleasantly surprised at how much I enjoyed the antique furniture posed in historically accurate rooms. Even though it houses several famous and classical pieces of renaissance art, it never seems too crowded to truly immerse yourself in the experience.
